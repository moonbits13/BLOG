LRT LINE 2

The Light Rail Transit Line 2 (LRT-2), also known as the Purple Line, is a rapid transit line in Metro Manila, Philippines, operated by the Light Rail Transit Authority (LRTA). Spanning 17.6 kilometers, it runs in an eastâ€“west direction from Recto Station in Manila to Antipolo Station in Rizal province. The line serves key cities including Manila, San Juan, Quezon City, Marikina, Pasig, and Antipolo, providing a vital transportation link across these urban areas. LRT-2 commenced operations in 2003, with its most recent extension to Antipolo opening in 2021 .

Despite being referred to as a light rail system, LRT-2 utilizes heavy rail metro cars that are longer and wider than those used on other lines in the country. These trains are designed for higher passenger capacity and comfort, featuring air conditioning, automated train operations, and accessibility features for passengers with disabilities. The line operates with a maximum speed of 60 km/h, and trains run at intervals of 8 to 10 minutes, with shorter intervals during peak hours .
